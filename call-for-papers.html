Call for Papers

Software developers and engineers have always been social creatures, indeed the new electronic communication and collaboration technologies of the last several decades (email, bulletin boards, Usenet, IRC, the web) were most often first colonized by programmers and engineers. Now software engineers are busy inventing (and simultaneously consuming) a whole new generation of social software and collaboration tools. Understanding this new social software engineering ecosystem is becoming an ever more important topic for software engineering research and crucial for software engineering practice. In this workshop, we plan to bring together researchers and practitioners working on different aspects of collaboration and knowledge sharing in software engineering as well as the engineering of Social Software to discuss new results and future research challenges.

Workshop Goals

  • Charting the social software engineering landscape. Collecting an overview of the latest developments with regard to the social aspects of software engineering and the use of social techniques and technologies in software development.
  • Assessing the impact on software engineering and engineers. Investigating how social software is influencing and impacting on software engineering practice and software engineers. What does it mean for the future of practice and practitioners?
  • Leveraging social software engineering. Provide pointers on how organizations and individuals can develop and incorporate social software technologies and techniques into their products and processes.

Workshop Topics

Major topics addressed at the workshop include (but not limited to):

Social Aspects in Software Engineering

  • Radically distributed software engineering and the role of social software
  • Relationship between users and developers, user feedback, mashups, and perpetual beta
  • Interaction and communication design in collaborative software engineering
  • Research methods, models, and tools supporting the social nature of software engineering
  • Exploiting and tailoring existing software engineering paradigms to support social software development
  • Analyzing the use of social network sites to connect software development communities

Implications of Social Software Engineering

  • Impact of social software engineering on teams, tools and processes
  • The social software engineer: redefining the software engineer, risks, rewards, and opportunities
  • Using social software to teach software engineering, teaching social aspects of software engineering
  • Leveraging social software to design, develop, test, and maintain software
  • Futuristic scenarios and research agendas

Engineering Social Software

  • Requirements engineering for Social Software
  • Engineering privacy, trust, and security for Social Software
  • Web 2.0, and Social Semantic Web applications
  • Analysis of particular and emerging challenges intrinsic to Social Software
  • Using social networking sites to understand, promote, and incentivize harmonious interactions between software development communities with different interests and goals
  • Novel technologies and novel applications of existing technologies for software engineering-related social network sites, including but not limited to open platform architectures, social network analysis, and automated analysis of shared content in social networks
